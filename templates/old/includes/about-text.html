About the Site

The Anthologist shares the simple mission of all anthologies: to expose curious readers to exquisite writing and engrossing ideas.

What Makes the Anthologist Unique?

There are many other sites that publish public domain texts; but several features distinguish the Anthologist from the rest. Pointing out these differences might be the best way to explain the site's purpose. [more ]

curation

Every one of the Anthologist's texts has been selected, intentionally, by a living, sentient human being, with feelings, who hopes that other readers might enjoy something that he himself enjoyed.

Nothing is added merely because it exists in the public domain, because it might garner more hits to the site, or because it is "a classic".

The Anthologist is not a public domain warehouse, and does not aspire to archival completeness or scholarly authority: It is a curated collection — not unlike a private art collection — made publicly available by the Powers of Internet.

readability

length

The Anthologist will not publish entire novels or treatises or multi-hundred-page monographs, won't link you to volumes you might think you should read but probably won't read. Instead, the Anthologist gathers complete short works and excerpts from longer works — then provides links so that you can continue reading, if you'd like, and finish the volume.

Consuming long books is good for you, of course; but there is no shame in reading a fantastic excerpt from The Anatomy of Melancholy instead of downloading the full elephantine bulk of it to your eReader, where it will languish until the end of time.

format

The Anthologist's selections are formatted to make online reading as pleasant as can be, with a clean layout and adjustable display, and without the clutter and wrack that repulses so many readers from online text.

ease of exploration

The Anthologist provides simple and straightforward means to follow-up on your interests, explore the collection, and discover new stuff worth reading. Most online collections can be browsed by author, maybe by date; at the Anthologist, you can browse by author, date, nation, original language, form, genre, context, topic, and style. You can also dig through highlights from the selections.

The Anthologist's tags connect selections, forming paths that readers can follow from one text to another.

the feed

Diversify your web-reading diet by adding the Anthologist to your RSS feeds. Alongside the latest celebrity gossip and apocalypse predictions, you'll receive classics and curiosities in the public domain as they are added to the Anthologist's collection.

(To find other sites that do other things with the public domain, have a look at the links on the resources page.)

Who's Behind the Anthologist?

The Anthologist is the work of one of the world's David Clarks (... parvus sum, nullus sum, altum nec spiro, nec spero). [more ]

He was led to this labor by his own idiosyncratic reading habits, academic studies, and teaching experience — by years of seeking out delightful, edifying, and unusual works; developing an appreciation for the aesthetics of ages past; engaging more and more with electronic texts; hunting for freely-reproducible, high-quality readings to feed his students; and always aspiring to keep track, somehow, of the best stuff he'd found, so he could re-read it himself and share it with others.

This David Clark is just one man. So he could use your help, if you're interested in getting involved. All aspects of the site would benefit from more input: the text collection, the programming, the design, the cataloging. Please contribute if you're interested.
